Global Agent Payment Enablement Program
NDA-safe case study draft focused on delivery outcomes and product leadership

This program focused on enabling a scalable payment journey for education agents, improving how agent-driven tuition flows are initiated, tracked, and reconciled across operational teams. The scope covered journey redesign, operational clarity, and delivery of a production-ready platform capability for agent payments.

As Product Owner, I led discovery, roadmap definition, stakeholder alignment, release planning, and rollout governance from initial requirements to adoption.

Problem Space
Business Challenges
  • Inconsistent agent payment journeys across regions and partner operating models.
  • Limited transparency for operations teams during payment lifecycle tracking.
  • Manual handling overhead in exception and follow-up scenarios.
  • Need for stronger consistency between commercial and operational expectations.
Product Goals
  • Create a predictable and scalable agent payment flow.
  • Improve operational visibility and handoff quality.
  • Reduce avoidable manual interventions.
  • Enable controlled rollout with measurable adoption checkpoints.
My Role as Product Owner
Leadership Scope
  • Owned end-to-end product planning and release direction.
  • Ran discovery workshops with business and operations stakeholders.
  • Prioritized scope into incremental milestones and delivery slices.
  • Facilitated alignment across commercial, compliance, operations, and engineering groups.
Execution Approach
  • Defined acceptance standards around business outcomes, not only feature output.
  • Used iterative demos and feedback loops to reduce delivery risk.
  • Maintained strict scope governance for go-live readiness.
  • Structured rollout communications and support playbooks for downstream teams.
Delivery Phases
Phase 1 — Discovery & Current-State Mapping Captured user journeys, key friction points, and operational dependencies.
Phase 2 — Product Design & Workflow Definition Defined target journey, decision points, and exception handling model.
Phase 3 — Build & Validation Delivered incremental capability slices with stakeholder reviews and UAT cycles.
Phase 4 — Launch & Adoption Released controlled rollout with enablement, monitoring, and refinement loop.
Outcomes (NDA-safe)
Business & Operational Impact
  • Standardized agent payment handling across participating regions.
  • Improved visibility for payment status tracking and ownership.
  • Reduced process ambiguity in cross-team execution paths.
  • Established a repeatable foundation for future enhancements.
Program Learnings
  • Early operational involvement significantly improves rollout quality.
  • Shared definitions and handoff rules prevent downstream friction.
  • Incremental rollout lowers risk while preserving delivery momentum.
  • NDA-safe storytelling should focus on decisions, outcomes, and governance.
