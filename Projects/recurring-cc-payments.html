1 Executive Summary

This project delivered a recurring card payments feature for accommodation-specific payment links on the cross-border payments platform. New regulatory requirements mandated that payers be able to set up automatic monthly or quarterly payments for housing fees. The solution integrated with an external payment provider's tokenization service to securely store and reuse card details without retaining sensitive data locally. The project spanned extensive scoping with legal, payments, and customer service teams, followed by phased development across the web platform, payer dashboard, operations integration, email automation, and failsafe mechanisms. A reworked requirements document addressed post-analysis additions including configurable plan durations and quarterly payment frequency. All deliverables passed internal and external testing before release.

4 Compressed Timeline

Key milestones across the delivery cycle

Week 1
Demand Request & Initial Scoping
Business leadership raised requirement for recurring accommodation payments. Initial scoping call held with payment provider to review tokenization capabilities and integration approach.
Weeks 2-3
Cross-Functional Analysis
Alignment calls with legal, payments team, customer service, and treasury. Key decisions made on payment frequency, fixed amounts, 12-month maximum, and card management approach.
Week 4
Concept Approval & Sign-Off
Business stakeholders approved the concept. BRD created covering use cases for setup, automatic payment, cancellation, and failure scenarios. Sign-off email attached.
Weeks 4-5
Legal & Terms Review
Legal team reviewed and revised terms and conditions for recurring payment types. Compliance check conducted on regulations requiring this feature for accommodation payment links.
Weeks 5-9
Core Development
Recurrence logic, payment selector, payer dashboard module, email automation, and terms and conditions integration developed. Shared form exclusion logic implemented to prevent feature leakage across links.
Weeks 9-10
Internal Testing & Validation
QA conducted internal testing across all deliverables. Parallel testing on shared form environments confirmed feature isolation. All test cases passed with no issues found.
Weeks 10-11
Reworked BRD & Post-Analysis Additions
Business requested configurable plan durations per institution, quarterly payment frequency option, and initial payment inclusion in plan count. Reworked BRD created and signed off.
Weeks 11-12
Failsafe & Operations Integration
Failsafe system for tokenization failures developed with automatic notification logic. Back office integration created to transfer recurring payment data to internal teams.
Week 12
External Testing & Release
External environment testing completed across all deliverables. Provider validation (penny testing) confirmed. Feature released to production on accommodation-specific links.

6 Scope Definition

In Scope

  • Payer dashboard with plan management and cancellation
  • Payment provider tokenization integration

Out of Scope

  • External API and partner integrations

  • Admin-side recurring plan management (deferred)
  • Recurring data in payer reports (identified post-release)

11 Lessons Learned

Early Cross-Functional Alignment Was Essential

Involving legal, payments, customer service, and treasury teams during the scoping phase prevented costly rework later. Key constraints (card types, country exclusions, T&C requirements) were surfaced before development began.

Comprehensive Testing Validated Quality

Running both internal and external test cycles for every deliverable, including parallel testing on shared-form environments, ensured zero issues at release and built confidence across all stakeholders.

BRD Rework Highlighted Scoping Gaps

The need for a fully reworked requirements document after initial analysis indicated that some business requirements (configurable durations, quarterly frequency, initial payment inclusion) should have been captured earlier. More structured workshops during scoping would help.

Admin Visibility Was Missed in Initial Scope

Post-release, QA identified that institution administrators had no visibility into recurring payment data within their reporting tools. This gap was flagged during testing but was not part of the original requirements, requiring a future improvement.
