Alternative Payment Method Provider Migration
Migrating Chinese alternative payment methods from a high-cost global processor to a regional payment provider — expanding payment options, reducing transaction fees, and enabling regulatory-compliant document collection across the education payments ecosystem.

This initiative addresses escalating processor fees that were eroding profitability on cross-border payments from China. By migrating to a regional provider with native APM support, the platform gains access to WeChat Pay, UnionPay Debit, and UnionPay Credit — payment methods previously unavailable to students. The integration introduces a complex exception engine governing which methods appear based on payment category, accommodation status, and specific partner configurations. Requested by a Senior Executive (International Commercial Director) and estimated at 48 developer-days across engineering and QA.

6 Overview
Executive Summary

This project migrates alternative payment methods for Chinese students from a high-cost global processor to a regional payment provider with native support for Alipay, WeChat Pay, and UnionPay variants. The initiative was requested by a Senior Executive due to escalating processor fees that were directly impacting transaction profitability.

The integration introduces a sophisticated exception engine that dynamically determines available payment methods based on payment category (tuition, accommodation, blocked accounts), partner-specific link configurations, and regulatory requirements. Students paying from China will be redirected to the provider's payment gateway and must complete document upload within 24 hours as part of Chinese regulatory compliance.

The scoping phase spanned 6 months from demand capture through formal hand-off, involving 8+ facilitated scoping calls, cross-team coordination between product, development, and the external provider, and a comprehensive BRD that maps every parameter, fee structure, and exception rule. Estimated at 24 days each for development and QA.

12 Learnings
Lessons & Insights
● What Worked Well

Extended Scoping Phase

Investing 4+ weeks in scoping calls before involving the engineering team ensured requirements were mature and well-understood. The development team received a comprehensive BRD with all edge cases documented rather than discovering them mid-implementation.

● What Worked Well

Exception Engine Approach

Designing a configurable exception engine with accommodation flags, link ID arrays, and exception-to-exception rules creates a maintainable system. Future payment method changes can be made through configuration rather than code modifications.

● Challenge

Multi-Method Complexity

Splitting UnionPay into three distinct variants (Debit, Credit, Credit/Debit) with different fee structures and category eligibility created a complex matrix. Every new partner configuration must be evaluated against all 5 methods across all categories.

● Key Learning

Regulatory Requirements Shape Architecture

Chinese document collection requirements fundamentally influenced the payment flow design. The redirect-based approach keeps compliance burden on the regional provider while the platform focuses on payment orchestration and method selection logic.

● Risk Identified

Provider Dependency for Document Collection

Relying on the regional provider's 24-hour document upload window introduces a dependency outside platform control. If the provider's document portal experiences downtime, payments could be left in limbo without platform-side mitigation options.

● Reflection

What I'd Do Differently

Would have engaged the business analyst earlier in the scoping phase rather than conducting all initial calls through product alone. Having the BA participate from week 2 would have reduced the hand-off gap and accelerated BRD authoring by several weeks, as requirements would have been captured in specification format from the start.
