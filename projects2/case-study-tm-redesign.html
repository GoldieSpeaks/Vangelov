Leading a comprehensive UX redesign of the student payment platform — overhauling login flows, payment forms, confirmation pages, and mobile responsiveness across hundreds of active payment links before the peak payment season.

The existing student payment journey suffered from poor usability, inconsistent design language, and excessive white space that confused users during the payment process. 6 Overview
Executive Summary

This project delivered a comprehensive UX and visual redesign of the education payment platform's student journey. The existing interface suffered from inconsistent styling, poor mobile support, and confusing user flows that impacted the payment experience for students across hundreds of institutional payment links.

Working with a dedicated UI/UX designer, the team identified a phased approach to overhaul 7 core page types — from a new greeting/gateway page, through login and registration redesigns, to completely reworked payment forms and confirmation pages — all without modifying the underlying system logic.

The redesign was released to production and deployed across all active payment links. The project constraint — delivering before payment season — was met successfully. Post-release testing validated the implementation across multiple link configurations.

12 Learnings
Lessons & Insights
● What Worked Well

Minimal-Disruption Approach

By constraining the redesign to visual and UX changes only, with zero functional modifications, the team eliminated the risk of payment processing regressions. This allowed confident deployment across hundreds of active links simultaneously.

● What Worked Well

Isolated Test Environment

Setting up a dedicated test link allowed the team to validate all changes in isolation before production deployment. This caught responsive design issues, logo alignment problems, and form interaction bugs before any student encountered them.

● Challenge

Multiple Custom Configurations

Each institutional payment link had its own customisations, making it complex to ensure the redesign worked consistently across all variations. The all-active-links report was essential for understanding deployment scope and identifying edge cases.

● Key Learning

Browser Compatibility Gaps

The Google toolbar issue with the fixed header was an unexpected compatibility gap that required additional investigation and resolution. Early cross-browser testing would have surfaced this sooner in the development cycle.

● Risk Identified

Seasonal Deadline Pressure

The hard requirement to deploy before payment season created time pressure that required deferring some polishing items (email templates, iframe rework) to post-release. While the core was delivered on time, the deferred items created follow-up work.

● Reflection

What I'd Do Differently

Would have included email template redesign in the initial scope rather than deferring it. The visual disconnect between the modernised web experience and the unchanged email templates created an inconsistency that the deferred approach only delayed resolving.
