6 Overview
Executive Summary

This project delivered a complete redesign of the platform's transactional email notification system. Over years of incremental development, notifications had accumulated inconsistent styling, outdated content, and missing information — creating a fragmented user experience that undermined brand credibility.

The rework covered payment notifications (bank transfer and card/APM), welcome emails, forgotten password flows, refund confirmations, agent registrations, payment reminders, rate expiry notices, account setup emails, and regulatory templates for specific jurisdictions. Each template was redesigned in Figma, specified with detailed acceptance criteria, and implemented with cross-client rendering validation.

The project also addressed compliance requirements by correcting Terms of Use links across all templates per legal team direction, and deprecated custom client exception templates in favour of a standardised approach with configurable logo placement.

12 Learnings
Lessons & Insights
● What Worked Well

Figma-First Design Specifications

Creating complete visual designs in Figma before development began eliminated ambiguity. Developers had pixel-perfect references with exact CSS values, and acceptance criteria could simply state "match the Figma." This reduced revision cycles significantly.

● What Worked Well

Granular Sub-Task Decomposition

Breaking 23 templates into individual sub-tasks with independent specifications enabled parallel tracking, clear ownership, and precise progress reporting. Each template could be developed, tested, and released independently without blocking others.

● Challenge

Template Discovery Was Non-Trivial

The initial mapping of all existing notifications took longer than expected. Templates were scattered across database-stored content, server-side files, and code-assembled components. A centralised template registry would have reduced the analysis phase.

● Key Learning

Legal Compliance as Ongoing Maintenance

Terms of Use link corrections surfaced post-release when legal reviewed the templates. Building compliance link validation into the initial acceptance criteria (rather than as a follow-up) would have avoided an additional corrective task cycle.

● Risk Identified

Custom Client Exceptions Create Long-Term Debt

The existence of custom templates for specific clients created ongoing maintenance burden and prevented unified updates. The decision to deprecate these in favour of configurable standard templates resolved a multi-year technical debt issue.

● Reflection

What I'd Do Differently

Would establish a design system component library for email templates from the start rather than designing each template individually. Shared components (headers, footers, buttons, legal blocks) would have reduced design time and ensured automatic consistency across future templates.
