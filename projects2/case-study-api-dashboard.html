6 Overview
Executive Summary

This project delivered a new self-service Payment Analytics Dashboard for thousands of API-integrated clients across the education payments ecosystem. Prior to this initiative, these clients could only access payment data programmatically — they had no visual analytics, no reporting tools, and no self-service dashboard. This placed the platform at a competitive disadvantage that was directly contributing to client churn.

The project moved through demand capture, wireframing, BRD creation, front-end prototyping, back-end integration, penetration testing, MVP release, client feedback incorporation, and an ongoing Phase 2. The MVP was released and adopted by pilot clients within 14 months of the initial demand request. Penetration testing returned only 1 informational finding (ISO 27001 compliant).

Client feedback drove a design overhaul that senior stakeholders described as "so much cleaner." Phase 2 includes a disbursements feature projected to eliminate a process that currently requires 3 full-time staff at peak season. C-level approval was received from the COO, International Commercial Director, and CPO.

12 Learnings
Lessons & Insights
● What Worked Well

Phased MVP Strategy

Releasing an MVP early allowed the team to collect real client feedback before investing heavily in design polish. This ultimately led to a more informed design overhaul that received enthusiastic stakeholder approval rather than guessing at improvements in isolation.

● What Worked Well

Security-First Approach

Engaging a penetration testing vendor before the first release ensured ISO 27001 compliance and surfaced only one informational finding. This validated the development approach and gave stakeholders confidence in the platform's security posture from day one.

● Challenge

Scope Expansion Required Active Management

Post-MVP, the project scope expanded significantly with white-labelling, disbursements, accommodation providers, and year-over-year analytics. Each required separate BRD iterations. Active scope management prevented uncontrolled creep while still responding to genuine business needs.

● Key Learning

Data Model Alignment Needed Earlier

The mismatch between back-office client file IDs and API client structures created integration complexity. Earlier alignment workshops between the back-office and platform teams would have reduced friction and accelerated the integration phase.

● Risk Identified

Design Debt from MVP Speed

Prioritising functional delivery over visual polish in the MVP created design debt that required a full overhaul. While the phased approach was correct, the gap between "functional" and "production-quality" design was larger than anticipated and required dedicated effort to close.

● Reflection

What I'd Do Differently

Would have engaged a UX designer from the start rather than iterating on developer-built templates. The design overhaul validated that early investment in visual polish accelerates adoption. The cost of redesigning post-launch exceeded what upfront design investment would have required.
