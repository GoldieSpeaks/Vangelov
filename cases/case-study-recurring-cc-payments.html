Recurring Card Payments
I delivered automated recurring accommodation payments through secure card tokenization on a cross-border education payments platform — driving regulatory compliance while creating a self-service payment system for institutional clients.

I led the end-to-end delivery of this feature after new regulatory requirements mandated automatic monthly or quarterly payments for housing fees. I coordinated the integration with an external payment provider's tokenization service to securely store and reuse card details without retaining sensitive data locally. I managed extensive scoping across legal, payments, and customer service teams, followed by phased development across the web platform, payer dashboard, operations integration, email automation, and failsafe mechanisms.

5 Overview
Executive Summary

I delivered a recurring card payments feature for accommodation-specific payment links on a cross-border education payments platform. New regulatory requirements mandated that payers be able to set up automatic monthly or quarterly payments for housing fees, and I owned the solution end-to-end.

I coordinated the integration with an external payment provider's tokenization service to securely store and reuse card details without retaining sensitive data locally. I authored the reworked requirements document addressing post-analysis additions including configurable plan durations per institution and quarterly payment frequency.

All nine deliverables passed both internal and external testing. I released the feature to production with zero defects, supporting both monthly and quarterly payment frequencies with per-institution configuration.

Project Timeline

End-to-end delivery from regulatory demand through phased development to live release.

Week 1
Demand Request & Provider Review
Business leadership raised the requirement for recurring accommodation payments. I held the initial scoping call with the payment provider to review tokenization capabilities, flow design, and integration approach.
Weeks 2–3
Cross-Functional Analysis
I led alignment calls with legal, payments team, customer service, and treasury. I drove key decisions: fixed monthly amounts, 12-month maximum, accommodation links only, card management approach for expired cards.
Week 4
Concept Approval & BRD Creation
I secured concept approval from business stakeholders. I created the BRD covering use cases for setup, automatic payment, cancellation, and failure scenarios. I coordinated legal terms and conditions drafting.
Weeks 5–7
Core Development Phase 1
I oversaw development of recurrence logic and payment selector for the Payment Form. Payer dashboard module built with plan management, status display, and cancellation controls. Terms and conditions integration completed.
Weeks 7–8
Email Automation & Operations Integration
I specified seven email templates covering payment reminders, success confirmations, failure notifications, plan expiry alerts, and quarterly-specific messaging. I coordinated operations data transfer implementation.
Week 8
Shared Form Exclusion Logic
I defined conditional rendering requirements to isolate recurring features on shared payment forms. Links without the feature enabled remain unaffected. I confirmed feature isolation through parallel testing.
Weeks 9–10
Reworked BRD & Post-Analysis Additions
Business requested configurable plan durations per institution, quarterly payment frequency option, and initial payment inclusion in plan count. I authored the reworked requirements document and secured sign-off.
Week 10
Failsafe System Development
I specified tokenization failure monitoring requirements. I defined automatic email alerts to internal teams for unresolved failures after 3 days, and retry logic for unsuccessful payments with 2 additional attempts.
Weeks 11–12
Internal & External Testing
I coordinated testing of all nine deliverables on both internal and external environments. I validated feature isolation through parallel testing on shared form environments. Provider penny testing confirmed end-to-end flow.
Week 12
Production Release
I released the feature to production on accommodation-specific links. All tests passed with zero defects. I briefed the operations team on recurring payment processing workflows.

10 Decisions
Key Decisions
When Decision Type
Week 1 Restrict feature to accommodation-specific links only. Regulatory requirement applied specifically to housing payments; limiting scope reduced risk and complexity across the platform. Strategic
Week 2 Use external provider tokenization rather than local card storage. Sensitive card data stored entirely on provider side, eliminating local PCI compliance burden and reducing security risk. Technical
Week 3 Fixed monthly amount with 12-month maximum. After alignment with business leadership, confirmed that payers enter a fixed amount charged automatically each period for up to 12 months. Process
Week 5 Create separate form handling for shared link scenarios. Shared forms could leak recurring features to non-eligible links; conditional rendering isolated the feature without affecting other forms. Technical
Week 10 Include initial payment in recurring plan count. Post-analysis feedback showed original logic created an extra payment; reworked to match payer expectations and institution billing cycles. Process
Week 10 Make plan duration and frequency configurable per institution. Different institutions had different accommodation periods; per-link configuration avoided one-size-fits-all constraints. Strategic

12 Learnings
Lessons & Insights
● What Worked Well

Early Cross-Functional Alignment

Involving legal, payments, customer service, and treasury teams during the scoping phase surfaced constraints before development began. Key decisions on card management, liability, and payment frequencies were locked in before a single line of code was written.

● What Worked Well

Comprehensive Dual-Environment Testing

Running both internal and external test cycles for every deliverable, including parallel testing on shared-form environments, ensured zero issues at release and validated feature isolation across linked payment forms.

● Challenge

BRD Rework Highlighted Scoping Gaps

The need for a fully reworked requirements document after initial analysis indicated that some business requirements (configurable duration, quarterly frequency) should have been captured earlier. More structured workshops during initial scoping would have prevented the rework cycle.

● Key Learning

Provider Documentation Must Be Validated Early

Outdated technical documentation from the payment provider caused delays during integration planning. Direct validation calls with providers should be standard practice during initial scoping, not discovered mid-development.

● Risk Identified

Admin Visibility Gap Emerged Post-Release

Post-release QA identified that institution administrators had no visibility into recurring payment data within their reporting tools. This gap was not part of the original requirements and was flagged for a future phase, highlighting the need to include admin user stories in initial scoping.

● Reflection

What I'd Do Differently

Would have run a structured requirements workshop with all five cross-functional teams simultaneously rather than sequential calls. The serial alignment process took weeks and still produced gaps that required a BRD rework. A single intensive session would have been faster and more complete.
