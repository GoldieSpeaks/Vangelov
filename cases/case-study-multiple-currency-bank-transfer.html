5 Overview
Executive Summary

I enabled multiple currency options for bank transfer payments across the education payments platform. Previously, students could only pay via a single default currency pair per country. I identified that competitors already offered this capability, creating a direct barrier to new client acquisition cited by the sales team.

I coordinated extensive cross-team alignment spanning Payments, Treasury, Customer Support, and CRM teams. I drove the BRD through three major revision cycles as the proposed logic evolved from a simple alternative currency flag to a comprehensive payment group architecture supporting redirect models and dynamic validation. I strategically sequenced development after infrastructure upgrades (AWS migration and PHP upgrade) to avoid rework.

I deployed the solution across API clients first, with subsequent expansion to WEB (college links). Coverage spans 35+ countries across Europe, Nordics, and Asia-Pacific including Austria, Germany, France, UK, Switzerland, Hong Kong, and more.

12 Learnings
Lessons & Insights
● What Worked Well

Infrastructure-Aware Sequencing

Deferring development until after AWS migration and PHP upgrade prevented building on unstable foundations. The 3-month delay was far less costly than the rework that would have been required if development started on the old infrastructure.

● What Worked Well

Cross-Team Scoping Calls

Regular alignment calls with Payments, Treasury, and CRM teams surfaced integration requirements early. The CRM payment group needs and treasury margin configurations were identified months before development, avoiding late-stage surprises.

● Challenge

BRD Instability Across Revisions

Three major BRD revisions created stakeholder fatigue and re-sign-off overhead. The scope evolved significantly between V1 and V3 as technical realities surfaced. Earlier technical feasibility analysis would have stabilised the specification sooner.

● Key Learning

Project Merging Requires Clear Governance

Merging the multi-currency project with bank transfer separations made technical sense but complicated tracking and accountability. Establishing clear ownership boundaries and combined milestone definitions at merge time would have improved clarity.

● Risk Identified

Cross-System Complexity

Changes spanning WEB, API, and CRM simultaneously created integration risk. Each system had different release cycles and team ownership. A shared integration test environment and coordinated release window protocol would reduce this risk for future cross-system projects.

● Reflection

What I'd Do Differently

Would invest in a technical spike before BRD V1 to understand system constraints upfront. The payment group architecture and redirect logic were discovered mid-process, forcing multiple rewrites. A 1-week technical analysis phase would have saved months of iteration.
