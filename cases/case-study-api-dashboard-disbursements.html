5 Overview
Executive Summary

I delivered Phase 2 of the API Dashboard — a Disbursements module that gives clients full visibility into all payouts made to their accounts. Prior to this initiative, payout information was only available through manually generated daily reports, requiring dedicated staff to prepare, summarise, and distribute reconciliation data.

I designed the solution: automated batching logic on the back-office side (grouping payments by beneficiary, account number, and currency), cross-system data transfer with status tracking, and a front-end disbursements page on the client dashboard. Clients can filter by date, beneficiary, payment channel, and reference — then drill down into individual transactions within each disbursement.

I drove the BRD through three major versions with significant schema and logic changes. I coordinated development across two databases (back-office and web), a data transfer pipeline, and front-end dashboard implementation.

12 Learnings
Lessons & Insights
● What Worked Well

Iterative BRD Process

Three major BRD versions with inline Confluence comments and dedicated review meetings ensured the final specification accurately reflected business needs. Early iterations caught schema issues that would have been expensive to fix post-development.

● What Worked Well

Detailed Sub-Task Breakdown

Creating four separate sub-tasks with full acceptance criteria and BRD section references gave developers clear, independent work units. Each can be developed, tested, and deployed incrementally without blocking others.

● Challenge

Cross-System Coordination Complexity

The project spans two databases, two development teams (CRM and web), and a corporate treasury trigger. Coordinating the data transfer handshake required multiple alignment sessions and clear ownership boundaries.

● Key Learning

Batching Rules Need Edge Case Documentation

Dynamic beneficiaries, global accounts, and allocated beneficiaries all required explicit exclusion rules. These edge cases were not obvious in initial requirements and only surfaced through treasury team collaboration.

● Risk Identified

Transfer Pipeline Single Point of Failure

If the data transfer process fails, clients see stale data without awareness. The status tracking mechanism (0/10/1) mitigates data loss but does not alert clients to delays. Monitoring and alerting may need to be added post-MVP.

● Reflection

What I Would Do Differently

Would have involved the treasury team earlier in BRD v1 to avoid the major schema overhaul in v1.3. The batching logic fundamentally changed after treasury provided operational context that was not captured in the initial demand request.
