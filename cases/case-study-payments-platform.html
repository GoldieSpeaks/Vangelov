INSEAD Degree Payments Platform
I led the end-to-end delivery of a bespoke multi-phase payments platform for INSEAD — from defining the API specification in client workshops through to coordinating dual penetration testing and live release, generating 1M+ EUR in recurring annual revenue.

I delivered a bespoke tuition payment solution for INSEAD's degree programmes, integrating with their student management system to support instalment-based payment plans, dual-payer models (students and corporate sponsors), multi-currency processing, and real-time webhook status notifications. The platform spans a custom enterprise API (JWT RS256 auth, batch processing, 4 environments, OpenAPI documentation), a purpose-built web portal, CRM integration, and full security certification.

5 Overview
Executive Summary

I delivered a fully bespoke payments platform for an enterprise education client operating globally across multiple campuses. The client required deep system integration with their student financial management system, supporting complex instalment-based payment plans that could be fulfilled by both students and third-party corporate sponsors.

I coordinated four parallel workstreams: (1) API development with JWT authentication, batch processing, and OpenAPI documentation; (2) a custom multi-currency web portal with student and sponsor dashboards; (3) CRM integration for back-office payment processing; and (4) compliance infrastructure including penetration testing, RAID logging, and automated notifications.

I delivered the project with transactions processing live and the client confirming satisfaction despite initial commercial pressures. Regular B2B calls and transparent tracking were cited as key enablers of the positive outcome.

9 Decisions
Key Decisions
When Decision Type
Sep 2024 Use existing student web-login infrastructure. Rather than building from scratch, leveraged the existing login system and created new login types (type 30 for students, type 31 for sponsors) to minimise development risk. Technical
Feb 2025 Keep refunds offline for MVP. Too much development complexity to include refund processing in the initial release. Deferred to post-MVP phase to maintain delivery timeline. Strategic
Mar 2025 Extend partial payment holding period to 30 days. Custom logic for this client only, overriding the standard 14-day auto-cancellation rule for split payment lines. Process
Apr 2025 Deletion endpoint via status flag rather than hard delete. Installments marked with "Delete" status flag instead of physical removal — preserving audit trail and enabling reconciliation. Technical
May 2025 I took ownership of all UX design deliverables directly. No dedicated designer was available, so I produced all wireframes and low-fidelity designs to maintain delivery velocity and eliminate handoff delays. Process

11 Learnings
Lessons & Insights
● What Worked Well

Regular B2B Calls & Trackers

Regular business-to-business calls were cited as instrumental in maintaining alignment. Development trackers provided transparency across all team members and kept both sides accountable to delivery milestones.

● What Worked Well

Development Breakdown Structure

Creating a structured 4-workstream breakdown early in the project gave the entire team a shared understanding of the full development cycle. This prevented scope ambiguity and enabled parallel execution across API, Web, CRM, and internal tracks.

● Challenge

Unrealistic Commercial Expectations

Initial timelines set by the commercial team were unrealistic, creating excessive pressure. Improvising solutions on existing infrastructure with heavy customisations added stress. Clear boundary-setting between commercial promises and technical feasibility was needed earlier.

● Key Learning

Direct Communication Channels Are Essential

Not having direct communication channels with the client created delays while waiting for responses through intermediaries. Establishing direct lines earlier would have accelerated decision-making significantly across the multi-phase lifecycle.

● Risk Identified

Creative Freedom Mid-Development

Giving the client creative freedom to introduce new requirements mid-development caused scope instability. Clear requirement outlines and expected delivery timelines should have been enforced without accepting additional items for development mid-process.

● Reflection

What I'd Do Differently

I would have pushed for a dedicated designer from the outset. Without one, I absorbed all UX design work and unscoped changes were made under time pressure, creating potential design language inconsistencies. Despite this, I turned the project from a negative trajectory to a positive outcome.
