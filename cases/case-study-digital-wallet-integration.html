Payment Integration
Digital Wallet Payment Integration
I delivered Google Pay and Apple Pay checkout on a cross-border payments platform in 12 weeks, leveraging an existing gateway to achieve zero custom integration code and zero production defects.

I identified that the platform could offer digital wallet payments without building direct integrations. I chose an indirect approach via the payment gateway's hosted forms — meaning the wallets appeared automatically once enabled at the provider level. I reduced the project to three focused UI enhancements: country-specific logo display, Apple Pay domain verification, and device-based dynamic switching between wallets. I managed the BRD through 16 revisions before securing executive approval from two senior leaders within 24 hours of each other.

5 Overview
Executive Summary

I introduced Google Pay and Apple Pay as digital wallet payment options on an international cross-border payments platform. By leveraging out-of-the-box wallet features from an existing payment gateway provider, I delivered the capability with minimal custom code — focusing development effort on country-specific logo display, Apple Pay domain verification, and device-based dynamic switching between wallets.

I managed the BRD through 16 revisions across multiple contributors before securing executive approval from the COO equivalent and Commercial Director within 24 hours of each other. Development completed in under 4 weeks once started, with all three sub-tasks passing internal and external testing with zero defects.

Payers across supported countries can now use their preferred digital wallet at checkout, with the correct option surfaced automatically based on device type — achieving 100% pass rate across all test scenarios.

I assigned a business analyst to own the analysis phase through sign-off. I arranged and completed the implementation call with the payment provider's technical team.
Week 3
Integration Approach Alignment
I organised the alignment meeting with finance, development, and commercial stakeholders. We evaluated direct vs. indirect integration — I recommended the indirect approach via provider's hosted forms for minimal development effort.
Weeks 3 – 7
BRD Authored (16 Revisions)
I oversaw creation of a comprehensive business requirements document with use cases, functional and non-functional requirements, country support lists, and device-based logic specifications. I coordinated multiple contributors including analyst, team lead, and QA.

I sent the BRD for dual executive approval. First executive signed off same day; second followed within 24 hours. I requested provider activation immediately after.

Device-based dynamic switching implemented — one wallet visible on its native device only, mutual exclusivity enforced. I verified all three sub-tasks passed internal testing with zero issues.

12 Learnings
Lessons & Insights
● What Worked Well

Leveraging Existing Provider Capabilities

By choosing indirect integration via the payment gateway's hosted forms, the team avoided significant custom development. The wallets appeared automatically once enabled at the provider level, reducing the project to focused UI enhancements rather than a full integration build.

● What Worked Well

Fast Executive Decision-Making

The country display decision was resolved in under 10 minutes by email. Both executives responded immediately with a clear, aligned answer. This set a benchmark for efficient stakeholder engagement and prevented any schedule delays.

● Challenge

Parallel Workstream Coordination

Coordinating provider activation, BRD approvals, and development readiness required tight alignment across finance, product, and engineering. Proactive scheduling and clear ownership ensured no phase waited on another despite multiple teams being involved.

● Key Learning

BRD Thoroughness as Quality Gate

The BRD went through 16 revisions across multiple contributors before reaching executive sign-off. While thorough, this process ensured complete clarity on use cases, country lists, device logic, and edge cases before any code was written — contributing to the zero-defect outcome.

● Risk Identified

Manual Domain Verification Dependency

The domain verification file must be manually deployed to any new domain added to the platform. This creates a risk of Apple Pay silently failing on new domains if the deployment step is missed during onboarding — no automated alerting exists for this scenario.

● Reflection

What I'd Do Differently

Would have requested provider activation and test environment credentials in week one rather than waiting for BRD sign-off. The activation was a zero-risk action that could have run in parallel with analysis, potentially shaving two weeks off the overall timeline.
