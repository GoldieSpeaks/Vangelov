Escrow Service Platform
I designed and delivered a full escrow holding service from the ground up — spanning visa status management, fund holding logic, split-line processing, and multi-system integration across internal operations and client-facing platforms.

I led a multi-quarter initiative to build a holding mechanism for international education payments where funds could be retained until specific conditions were met — particularly around visa application outcomes. I mapped and delivered support for multiple visa statuses (Pending, Issued, Refused, Residence Permit, No Visa Required), each triggering different fund release or refund workflows. My work spanned process mapping, use case documentation, operations system development, and client-facing portal integration, with phased releases across two major platform components.

5 Overview
Executive Summary

I delivered the platform's first escrow holding service — a multi-quarter initiative where I led process mapping, system design, operations platform development, and client portal integration. The core business need: hold international education payment funds until visa application outcomes are known, then release to the institution or refund to the payer based on the outcome.

I designed a solution supporting five visa statuses (Pending, Issued, Refused, Residence Permit, No Visa Required), each with distinct fund handling rules. I architected split-line processing to enable individual tracking when a single payment covers multiple beneficiaries. I sequenced delivery so the operations system was built first to establish the workflow foundation, followed by the client-facing portal integration enabling institutional self-service.

The escrow service I delivered transformed a previously unscalable manual process into a systematic platform capability — supporting the company's expansion into markets where visa-contingent education payments are the norm.

10 Decisions
Key Decisions
When Decision Type
Q3 2023 Operations-first delivery sequence. Build the internal operations platform before the client portal. Establishes the workflow foundation and allows operations teams to validate business rules before exposing to external users. Strategic
Q3 2023 Five discrete visa statuses over binary hold/release. Granular status model (Pending, Issued, Refused, Res. Permit, No Visa Required) enables distinct fund handling rules per scenario rather than a simple hold-or-release binary. Technical
Q3 2023 Split-line architecture for multi-beneficiary payments. Individual fund tracking per student within a single payment enables partial releases when some visa applications succeed while others remain pending. Technical
Q4 2023 Manual fund release over full automation. Compliance and risk considerations required human verification before fund release. Automated triggers notify operations teams but do not execute releases without manual confirmation. Process
Q1 2024 Stabilization quarter before portal integration. Deliberately paused new feature development to refine the operations system based on live usage patterns. Ensured the foundation was solid before building the client-facing layer on top. Strategic
Q2 2024 Client self-service visa updates over operations-mediated. Allowing institutions to directly update visa statuses reduced operations team workload and improved data freshness. Validation rules prevent invalid transitions. Process

12 Learnings
Lessons & Insights
● What Worked Well

Operations-First Sequencing

Building the internal operations system first created a validated foundation before exposing functionality to external clients. Operations teams discovered edge cases in live usage that were resolved before the client portal was built — preventing costly rework and poor client experiences.

● What Worked Well

Stakeholder Recording Sessions

Recording multiple sessions with operations stakeholders captured nuances that would have been lost in written requirements alone. The recordings became reference material throughout development, reducing ambiguity and preventing the "that's not what we meant" cycle.

● Challenge

Multi-Quarter Scope Management

An 8-quarter programme spanning multiple teams required constant scope discipline. Stakeholder expectations evolved over the programme duration, and each phase's delivery created appetite for additional features. Maintaining focus on the core escrow capability required active boundary management.

● Key Learning

Stabilization Quarters Pay Dividends

The deliberate decision to pause new features after the operations release and dedicate a full quarter to stabilization prevented technical debt accumulation. By the time the portal was built, the foundation was solid and the portal development moved significantly faster as a result.

● Risk Identified

Manual Release Dependency

Retaining manual fund release as a compliance requirement created an operational bottleneck as volumes grew. While appropriate for initial launch, the scaling limitation was visible early. Automated release with audit trail should be considered as transaction volumes increase.

● Reflection

What I'd Do Differently

Would have invested in a comprehensive status transition diagram and state machine specification upfront rather than documenting individual use cases separately. As the visa statuses and their fund handling rules became more complex, a single authoritative state machine would have prevented several interpretation differences between development and operations teams.
