5 Overview
Executive Summary

I delivered a self-service Payment Analytics Dashboard for thousands of API-integrated clients across the education payments ecosystem. These clients previously had no visual analytics, no reporting tools, and no self-service dashboard — only raw API endpoints. This competitive gap was directly causing client churn.

I drove the project through demand capture, wireframing, BRD co-authoring, front-end prototyping, back-end integration oversight, penetration testing coordination, MVP release, client feedback synthesis, and a design overhaul. The MVP went live within 14 months. Penetration testing returned only 1 informational finding (ISO 27001 compliant).

I gathered client feedback that drove a design overhaul senior stakeholders described as “so much cleaner.” Phase 2 includes a disbursements feature projected to eliminate a process requiring 3 full-time staff at peak season. I secured C-level approval from the COO, International Commercial Director, and CPO.

12 Learnings
Lessons & Insights
● What Worked Well

Phased MVP Strategy

Releasing an MVP early allowed the team to collect real client feedback before investing heavily in design polish. This ultimately led to a more informed design overhaul that received enthusiastic stakeholder approval rather than guessing at improvements in isolation.

● What Worked Well

Security-First Approach

Engaging a penetration testing vendor before the first release ensured ISO 27001 compliance and surfaced only one informational finding. This validated the development approach and gave stakeholders confidence in the platform's security posture from day one.

● Challenge

Scope Expansion Required Active Management

Post-MVP, the project scope expanded significantly with white-labelling, disbursements, accommodation providers, and year-over-year analytics. Each required separate BRD iterations. Active scope management prevented uncontrolled creep while still responding to genuine business needs.

● Key Learning

Data Model Alignment Needed Earlier

The mismatch between back-office client file IDs and API client structures created integration complexity. Earlier alignment workshops between the back-office and platform teams would have reduced friction and accelerated the integration phase.

● Risk Identified

Design Debt from MVP Speed

Prioritising functional delivery over visual polish in the MVP created design debt that required a full overhaul. While the phased approach was correct, the gap between "functional" and "production-quality" design was larger than anticipated and required dedicated effort to close.

● Reflection

What I'd Do Differently

Would have engaged a dedicated UX designer from the start to elevate wireframes into high-fidelity designs. The design overhaul validated that early investment in visual polish accelerates adoption. The cost of redesigning post-launch exceeded what upfront design investment would have required.
