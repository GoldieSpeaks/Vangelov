Data Deletion & Retention

Project Overview

The Data Deletion Initiative is a bi-annual project designed to identify and remove sensitive personal data that has remained untouched for over 10 years. This proactive measure ensures the protection of user privacy and complies with industry-standard regulations, which mandate the deletion of outdated data across all companies and large organisations handling user information.

The goal of this project was to precisely align with these regulatory requirements by identifying eligible user data and securely erasing it to safeguard privacy and maintain compliance.

Solution Overview

Identifying the appropriate approach.

Time for Execution

At first glance, data deletion may seem straightforward, especially when considered from a high-level perspective. However, upon closer inspection, the process involves a multitude of complexities, particularly when dealing with legacy systems that were not originally designed with modern privacy standards, like GDPR, in mind. These systems often accumulate years of sensitive user information from a variety of sources, creating layers of interconnected data that must be carefully identified, mapped, and managed before deletion can occur.

One of the primary challenges in this process is that sensitive data isn't always stored in easily identifiable locations. User data can be spread across multiple channels, integrated into various parts of the database, or intertwined with other non-sensitive information, making its identification more complex. Moreover, evolving privacy regulations and compliance requirements impose additional constraints, increasing the pressure to ensure that all necessary data is correctly pinpointed and erased.

Our approach addresses these complexities head-on, breaking the process down into three primary phases that simplify execution. Each step requires careful planning and specialized algorithms to ensure precision. Even though these steps may initially seem straightforward, they demand a deep understanding of both the database architecture and the applicable regulatory frameworks. This ensures not only successful data deletion but also minimizes the risk of accidental data loss or incomplete deletion, both of which could lead to non-compliance or future complications.
