E Executive Summary

This project replaced the existing third-party payment processing partner for South Korean payments with a new, more cost-effective provider. The previous partnership suffered from high transaction fees and slow processing/settlement times, prompting a strategic switch to a provider offering competitive rates and faster settlement.


Key outcome: The integration followed a static account approach, replacing the existing provider's framework within the payment flow. When payers select South Korea as their payment origin, they now see the new provider's account details — resulting in lower fees, faster processing, and an improved commercial position for both the platform and its clients.

💡 Lessons Learned

What Went Well

Static Account Simplicity

The static account approach significantly reduced integration complexity compared to a full API-to-API provider migration, enabling a cleaner and faster transition.

Key Learning

Provider Evaluation Should Be Continuous

The fee and settlement issues with the previous provider accumulated over time. Regular provider benchmarking could have identified the need for a switch earlier.

Caution

Dual-Channel Coordination Required

Replacing a provider across both web and API channels requires coordinated releases to ensure payers on both platforms receive consistent account details simultaneously.

Risk Identified

Provider Dependency Concentration

Switching from one provider to another for a single country still concentrates risk. A fallback or dual-provider strategy could provide resilience for future country-specific integrations.
