E Executive Summary

This project created an entirely new web payment portal model for a major academic publisher, enabling individuals, companies, agencies, and payers to purchase cross-borderal materials through a dedicated payment experience. Unlike existing payment flows designed for tuition, this portal introduced product-based payment logic where products map to different beneficiary bank accounts, with card payment support and local payment method integration for markets including Brazil and Colombia.


Key outcome: A fully customized payment portal with a new web login role, custom form design, multi-beneficiary product routing, reworked notifications (removing payer-specific references), staff login with payment history, and integration with local payment providers for emerging markets.

💡 Lessons & Insights

What Worked Well

Clean-Slate Portal Design

The decision to build a new portal model rather than adapting existing tuition payment flows proved correct. It avoided logic conflicts and created a reusable template for future non-cross-border payment use cases.

Challenge

Mid-Project Scope Pivot

The project fundamentally changed direction after initial scoping, moving from adapting existing agent flows to creating a completely new portal model. This required re-engaging all stakeholders and restarting the design process.

Key Learning

Notification De-Coupling

Removing payer-specific references from payment instructions and confirmation emails required creating new notification templates per country. Future portal variants should plan for notification customization from the start.

Risk Identified

Payment Provider Compatibility

Not all existing payment providers were compatible with agent-type payments. Validation calls were needed with each provider (local pay, card gateway, regional processors) to confirm operational feasibility before development.
